What is 'Algorithmic Trading' and How Does it Shape the Market?

The majority of trades today aren't made by humans. Dive into the world of algorithmic trading to understand the forces driving modern financial markets.

Algorithmic trading (or "algo-trading") is a method of executing orders using automated, pre-programmed trading instructions accounting for variables such as time, price, and volume. It's a broad term that covers everything from simple strategies to highly complex artificial intelligence systems.

Why Use Algorithms?

Algorithms have several key advantages over human traders:

  • Speed: Algorithms can analyze markets and execute trades in microseconds, a speed impossible for humans.
  • Accuracy: They eliminate the risk of manual errors, like typing the wrong number of shares.
  • No Emotion: Algorithms stick to their programming and are immune to the fear and greed that can plague human decision-making.
  • Backtesting: Strategies can be rigorously tested on historical data to see how they would have performed before risking any real capital.
"The machine does not beat you. It's the developer of the machine who beats you." - A quant trader

How Does This Relate to Anomaly Detection?

The rise of algorithmic trading is a major reason why anomaly detection is so crucial. A bug in a trading algorithm or a "flash crash" caused by interacting algorithms can generate massive, unexpected market moves. These are precisely the types of system-driven anomalies that StockPulse is designed to flag.

Understanding the algorithmic landscape is key to contextualizing the data we see. When an anomaly is detected, it could be a sign of human manipulation, a reaction to news, or the result of a rogue algorithm. By using data science to monitor the market's pulse, we can better understand the complex ecosystem we operate in.
