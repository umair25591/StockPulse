Decoding the Candlesticks: A Visual Guide to Reading Stock Charts

Those little red and green bars on a stock chart are packed with information. Learn how to read candlestick charts to understand market sentiment at a glance.

For any trader, a candlestick chart is a fundamental tool. Each "candle" represents a specific time period (like a day or an hour) and tells a story about the battle between buyers (bulls) and sellers (bears) during that time.

The Anatomy of a Candlestick

Every candlestick has four key data points:

  • The Open: The price at the start of the period.
  • The Close: The price at the end of the period.
  • The High: The highest price reached during the period.
  • The Low: The lowest price reached during the period.

The main rectangular part is called the "real body." The lines extending above and below are the "wicks" or "shadows."

What Do They Tell Us?

The size and shape of the body and wicks reveal a lot about market sentiment. A long green body suggests strong buying pressure. A long wick on top of a small body suggests that buyers tried to push the price up, but sellers pushed it back down, indicating potential resistance.

By learning to recognize common patterns like "Doji," "Hammer," or "Engulfing" patterns, traders can gain valuable insights into potential trend reversals or continuations.
